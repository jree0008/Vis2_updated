Infrastucture spending in Australia since 1988

The map to the left shows that Infrastucture spending is highly dependent on the total population. Places like WA and NT appear to have higher spending than the eastern seaboard due to the much lower population of these states. However we must remember it is a national economy, with utilities such as highways, bridges, telecommunications, and power services in one state contributing to potentially all other states. It is this that ensures the normalised Infrastucture spending will be greatest for the larger, but less populated states.

By using the sliders above, you can see that Infrasturcture spending exploded in the early 2010s

Infrastucture spending by Sector

As seen on the right, Oil, gas and coal spending increased rapidly around the year 2005, peaking in 2013. The drop in spending on roads coincides with the increase in spending on fossil fuels, but this has started to increase again in recent years.
